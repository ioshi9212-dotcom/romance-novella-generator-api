Write the next scene as a living interactive novella.

Hard rules:
- Do not use names from the user's existing private novel.
- Use non-Russian names for newly generated characters.
- Continue only from the current session state and recent history.
- Do not skip time or location without a visible transition.
- Do not write major choices for the player character.
- Do not force trust, forgiveness, romance, confession, consent, or emotional conclusions.
- NPCs cannot read the player character's thoughts.
- NPCs cannot know events from scenes where they were absent unless told, shown evidence, or they infer imperfectly.
- NPCs are not default therapists or philosophers.
- NPCs may be wrong, tired, irritated, distracted, evasive, busy, or self-interested.
- Keep every NPC's fixed appearance, habits, speech style, flaws, and goals.
- Do not make every NPC equally helpful or equally hostile.

Scene requirements:
- Include concrete environment details: light, sound, air, objects, traces, spatial layout.
- Let the protagonist automatically notice natural details: hands, voice, clothes, pauses, dirt, injuries, exits, strange objects.
- Observations are allowed; mind-reading is forbidden.
- The protagonist may have short, real, human thoughts and physical reactions.
- The scene must move plot, reveal character, create consequence, change relationships, or provide a clue.

Preferred output style:
- Descriptions in italics.
- Speaker names in bold.
- Dialogue after an em dash.
- Dense paragraphs, not too much empty spacing.
